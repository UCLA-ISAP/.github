# UCLA Integrated Substance Use and Addiction Programs

ISAP is a research, training, and clinical program within the [Department of Psychiatry and Biobehavioral Sciences](https://www.semel.ucla.edu/) at the David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA. We study the prevention, treatment, and consequences of substance use disorders, and work to move evidence-based approaches into practice.

## What We Do

**Research** — Our faculty lead federally funded studies on pharmacotherapy, epidemiology, health services, and policy evaluation related to substance use. We produce roughly 100 peer-reviewed publications per year.

**Training** — We run NIDA-funded pre- and postdoctoral fellowships in clinical addiction research, continuing education programs for providers, and international training initiatives with partners including SAMHSA and UNODC.

**Clinical Care** — Through the UCLA Addiction Medicine Clinic and Addiction Psychiatry Division, we provide direct treatment for substance use disorders and supervise trainees in clinical settings.
